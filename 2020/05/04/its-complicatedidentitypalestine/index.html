IT’S COMPLICATED IDENTITY PALESTINE

Sariyah Abuzant

The Struggle of Retaining the Identity of Palestine in the Past Century

This mixtape is about the many faces of the Palestinian identity over the past century. Working on the idea that identity is made up of the values and beliefs that a person or community hold dearest, it intends to explore how the values, hence the identity, of the Palestinians has been challenged. These challenges will be referenced through music, poetry and sound bites, found on-line and in literature, that have been influenced through the course of the political, cultural and technological influences. It will include tracks from local and diaspora sources, that represent how each has held on to the Palestinian identity despite the influences mentioned above.

Aims:

The aims of this mixtape are to help two kinds of audiences: Palestinians who need to evaluate the values and beliefs that make up their own identity, and for outsiders, to support the idea that the identity “Palestinian” has been imbedded in the history of this land, not just a term made up for “Arabs” to stake their claim on it. 

1. Show how Palestinian values have challenged over the past 100 years.

2. Show how these challenges have rooted Palestinian identity.

3. Show that although Palestinian identity has changed somewhat, Palestinians are still proud to claim their identity, and cling to their values, beliefs and culture despite outside influences and migration.

4. Show the identity that has remained unchanged throughout the years.

5. Show that despite attempts to erase Palestinian identity, there continues to be a resistance against it through new and unconventional means, such as Rap music and comedy, that instills how important it is for the younger generation to claim and project Palestinian identity, as a form of resistance.

6. Show that although there are millions of Palestinian refugees in the world, many of them who have taken on another nationality, still refuse to give up their Palestinian identity, and take great pride in it.

Research approaches/methodologies: 

For this mixtape we need to understand the struggles that have occurred, the changes they have created and the catalyst behind them. We need to link music, poetry, comedy, demonstrations etc. to the timeline of the resistance. This mixtape will be ordered chronologically, beginning in 1920’s until the present.
