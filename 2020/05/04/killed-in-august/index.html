KILLED IN AUGUST

Killed in August by Dima, Leyana, and Manar (DLM) is a mix-tape re-narrating the massacre of Tal Al-Zaatar, a genocide committed against thousands of Palestinians in Lebanon, and is considered a human atrocity against innocent civilians including women and children, which took place after the siege of the camp for 52 days forcing its residents to eat dogs, cats, and even dead bodies to avoid starvation. 


This mix-tape bundles together musical and non-musical tracks narrating the story of Tal Al-Zaatar. It begins with a song by Sheikh Imam under the name of Tal Al-Zaatar, which he composed to mourn the human atrocity and the betrayal of Palestinian refugees by their fellow Arabs, the perpetrators of the genocide motivated by religious bigotry that fueled the Lebanese Civil War, as well as their leaders who left them to face death and sacrificed more than 2000 civilian of the residents of the camp without giving it a second glance.


Following the documentary by the Syrian Political Encyclopedia which tells the political and the historical context of the massacre, Ahmad Azzatar -originally a poem by Mahmoud Darwish, is a track narrated by Marcel Khalifah in which he poetically describes the tragic events of the massacre, while the background music is a combination of a piece composed by Ziad Al Rahbani, additionally to violent sound effects mimicking those heard in wars, including bullets, bombing, and helicopters. The background music of this track portrays the struggle of refugees wherever they go, whether inside their homelands or in neighboring countries, they are subjected to discrimination, humiliation, and other horribles of war.
Lastly, we added a re-narration of the genocide, trying to conclude the chronicle order of events to make our audience aware of what the Palestinian refugees went through before, during, and after the massacre. We created this mix-tape to commemorate this very incident which we believe that no one should forget about and to represent the injustice which has become a part of refugees’ daily life.
